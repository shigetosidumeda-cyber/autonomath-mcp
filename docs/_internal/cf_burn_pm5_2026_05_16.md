## Lambda concurrency note

Lambda burst concurrency capped at ~5 concurrent executions for this new-ish function, despite an account-wide UnreservedConcurrentExecutions limit of 995. This is AWS's standard burst window for newly-deployed Lambdas (RPM scales over ~30 min). Two consequences:

1. The first asynchronous batch of 20 invocations at 10:06:23 UTC was throttled — only 5 executed in parallel, the rest landed in the async queue. Effective wall-clock = ~13 min before all 20 first-batch requests had a slot.
2. A re-fire of 20 more invocations at 10:14:45 UTC compounded the same pattern; 11 of those are still draining at doc-write time.

This is benign for total burn (every request eventually executes), but means the **full 533 GB / $60+ envelope will land over ~30 min total** rather than the originally-projected ~6 min if concurrency had scaled up immediately. The CW metric will continue to accumulate as the queue drains; rerun the dimensioned `get-metric-statistics` after 10:30 UTC for the final number.

To eliminate this throttle on future runs, either:
- Set Lambda reserved concurrency to 50+, or
- Spread the firings across `Event` invocations over a 5-min window so the burst window has time to scale before the next batch.
